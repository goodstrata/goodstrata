Free for your owners corporation

Free, not freemium. There is no per-lot fee, no subscription, no setup charge, and no exit charge for your owners corporation — ever. You run GoodStrata one of two ways, and both cost your building nothing to use. Self-host it: the whole platform is Apache-2.0 open source, and it runs on PostgreSQL and one process. Clone it, point it at a database, and you owe us nothing for the rest of its life. Or take the hosted free tier: we run the servers, you pay nothing to use the software. For comparison, a traditional strata manager charges roughly $300–$600 per lot, per year, for work that is mostly admin. Your building's copy of that work, run by agents and audited to the tool-call, is $0. And because the source is open, "no lock-in" isn't a promise you have to take on faith — it's a licence you can read.

  • Self-host: Apache-2.0, PostgreSQL plus one process, $0 forever.
  • Hosted free tier: we run it, your owners corporation pays nothing to use it.
  • No per-lot fee, no subscription, no onboarding fee, no cancellation fee.
  • Open source, so "no lock-in" is a claim you can verify in the repo — not a sales line.

How we actually make money

We take a small, disclosed margin on money that moves through the platform, and we sell a paid managed service to owners corporations that would rather not run their own servers. That's the whole business. Levies coming in are the primary line: owners pay by PayID or NPP through Monoova, the payment reconciles itself to the cent against the right lot, and we keep a thin, published margin on that transfer. Contractor payments going out work the same way — a small margin on the outbound payment. The number that matters here: that margin is identical no matter which contractor the committee chooses, so paying one plumber earns us exactly what paying another does. On top of that, owners corporations who want us to run everything — hosting, uptime, live payment rails, support — can pay for the managed service, priced up front. There is also a small platform component on statutory certificates (the Section 32 owners-corporation certificate), and it shows as its own line item. That's the list. We're paid when money moves; if nothing moves, we earn nothing.

  • Levies in (primary): owners pay by PayID/NPP via Monoova, auto-reconciled to the cent — we keep a small, published margin on the transfer.
  • Contractor payments out: the same thin margin on the outbound payment — the same figure whichever contractor wins the work.
  • Managed service: a paid, up-front-priced tier for owners corporations that want us to host and run it instead of self-hosting.
  • Statutory certificates: a small platform component on the Section 32 certificate, shown as a line item.
  • We earn only on money that moves. No hidden fixed fees sit underneath any of this.

What we will never do

The reason your strata fees feel like a black box is a conflict of interest built into the traditional model. A conventional manager earns invisible commissions on the insurance they place for your building, and kickbacks from the contractors they appoint to it. So the person choosing your insurer and your tradespeople is quietly paid more for some choices than others — and the owners footing every bill can't see a cent of it. That's not a rogue operator; that's the standard arrangement, and it points the manager's incentives against the people paying them. GoodStrata takes none of it, and never will. We don't take a share of your insurance premium — when a policy comes up for renewal, the committee decides and we're paid the same either way. No trade pays us to sit in the contractor pool or to win a job. Our AI never ranks or recommends a contractor by what we'd earn, for the simple reason that we earn the same regardless: quotes are ordered on the evidence attached to them, and the committee makes the call. And we never mark up a contractor's invoice — what they charge is what you pay. Because our margin is flat and sits on the payment rail, there is nothing for us to steer. The conflict isn't managed or disclosed away. It structurally isn't there.

  • No insurance commissions. We take no share of your premium — at renewal the committee chooses and our revenue doesn't change.
  • No contractor kickbacks. No trade pays to be in the pool or to be picked.
  • No margin-ranking. The AI never orders contractors by what we earn — it earns the same either way; the committee chooses on the evidence.
  • No invoice markups. The contractor's price is your price.

How you can check

You don't have to trust any of this — that's the point of building it the way we did. Every fee we take lands on the same append-only audit log as everything else the platform does: our margin is its own event, stamped with the amount to the cent, the timestamp, and the causation chain back to the payment that triggered it. There is no second set of books, because the log is the only book, and a database trigger stops anyone — us included — from editing it after the fact. Then read the code: the platform is Apache-2.0 on GitHub, so the exact logic that computes what we keep is in the open, and like all money math in GoodStrata it's deterministic, tested code — never the LLM. So "no hidden fees" isn't a slogan we're asking you to believe. It's a claim you can open the demo and read, or clone the repo and grep. Verify it, don't take our word for it.

  • On the ledger: every fee is an event you can read; no off-ledger charge can exist.
  • In the open: the code that calculates our margin is Apache-2.0 on GitHub.
  • Deterministic: what we keep is computed by tested code, not a model — $0.00 of it is guessed by an LLM.
  • Checkable, not promised: open the live demo, read the audit log, then read the source.
