How it works

Agents execute, you decide, and it's all on the record.

Traditional strata management hands one company your money, your maintenance, and your meetings, then asks you to trust it. GoodStrata splits the job in three: agents execute, humans decide, and every action is audited forever.

Agents execute

The routine work runs itself (levies, payment matching, arrears, repair triage, contractor dispatch, meeting notices) with a full transcript of every step.

Humans decide

Anything that spends money, commits the building, or bends a rule stops at the decisions inbox (evidence attached) and waits for a person to tap approve.

The money is code

Apportionment, penalty interest, reconciliation and vote weighting are deterministic, tested code. If every AI were a random number generator, your levies would still sum to the budget.

Pricing

Free for your strata: we earn only on payments that move.

The reason your strata fees feel like a black box is a conflict of interest built into the traditional model: the manager earns invisible commissions on the insurance they place and kickbacks from the contractors they appoint. GoodStrata takes none of it. Here is every dollar we take, and every dollar we refuse.

Earn How we actually make money

  • Levies in (primary). When live payments launch, owners will pay by PayID/NPP via Monoova, auto-reconciled to the cent. We will keep a small margin on the transfer, published as its own line before the first dollar moves.
  • Contractor payments out. The same thin margin on the outbound payment, identical whichever contractor the committee picks.
  • Managed service. A paid, up-front-priced tier for owners corporations that want us to host and run it instead of self-hosting.
  • Statutory certificates. A small platform component on the owners corporation certificate (OC Act s 151, attached to the vendors's s 32 statement), shown as its own line item.

Refuse What we will never do

  • No insurance commissions. We take no share of your premium. At renewal the committee chooses and our revenue doesn't change.
  • No contractor kickbacks. No trade pays to be in the pool or to be picked.
  • No margin-ranking. The AI never orders contractors by what we earn. It earns the same either way; the committee chooses on the evidence.
  • No invoice markups. The contractor's price is your price.

Because our margin is flat and sits on the payment rail, there is nothing for us to steer. The conflict isn't managed or disclosed away: it structurally isn't there. And every fee we take lands on the same append-only audit log as everything else.

Open source

Read every line. Export your data. Run it yourself.

GoodStrata is Apache-2.0 open source, top to bottom, not an open-core shell around a paid engine you can never see. The self-host floor is PostgreSQL and one Node process, and with a local model your building's money, minutes and maintenance never leave hardware you control.

  • Apache-2.0, the whole platform. The finance agent, arrears ladder, apportionment, decision gates and event log are all on GitHub: read them, fork them, audit the money math.
  • One command brings up a building. docker compose --profile app up starts Postgres and one container. No Kafka, no Redis, no managed cloud.
  • Local AI, nothing over the wire. Set AI_PROVIDER=local, point it at a model in Ollama: no API key, no data sent anywhere.
  • "No lock-in" is checkable. Read the code, export your ledger and event log in open formats, run it yourself: the licence guarantees it, forever.
