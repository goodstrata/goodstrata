## 1. Executive Summary — what "being a registered manager" requires

In Victoria, acting as the manager of an owners corporation **for fee or reward while unregistered is a criminal offence** (OC Act s 178, 60 penalty units). To operate lawfully, Good Strata Pty Ltd must clear four gates before it takes on a single paying scheme:

1. **Registration** with the Business Licensing Authority (BLA), via CAV's myCAV system — a company may register in its own name (OC Act Part 12, ss 178–199; CAV registration page).
2. **Professional indemnity (PI) insurance of at least $2 million**, held continuously, naming the company and the OC-management business — a hard precondition to both registration and to lawful appointment (OC Act s 119(5); OC Regs 2018 reg 10; CAV).
3. **Statutory trust accounts** — all OC money held on trust, held in **separate bank accounts for separate owners corporations** with only narrow exceptions (OC Act s 122(2)(c), s 122(3)).
4. **Two Commonwealth overlays** that turn on the platform's money-movement design: whether collecting/disbursing OC levies via Monoova makes GoodStrata a **non-cash-payment-facility issuer needing an AFSL** (Corporations Act ss 763D, 764A, 911A), and whether it is an **AML/CTF reporting entity** (AML/CTF Act 2006). Both are legal characterisations — **not** things the company can self-certify.

Registration, once granted, is **ongoing** (it is not annually renewed) but is **automatically cancelled** if PI insurance lapses, and carries continuing duties: approved-form appointment contracts capped at 3 years, per-OC trust accounting, commission disclosure, competitive procurement, and reporting to each OC's AGM.

## 3. BLA Registration (OC Act Part 12, ss 178–199)

> **Citation correction (important).** The compliance audit and task brief cite "s 119(2)" as the *"must be registered"* offence and treat Part 6 as the registration regime. That is a **misattribution**, now confirmed against primary text:
> - **s 178 (Part 12)** is the offence: *"A person must not, alone or in partnership, carry out any function as the manager of an owners corporation for fee or reward unless the person is registered under this Part. Penalty: 60 penalty units."* (verified verbatim, AustLII).
> - **s 119(2)** is the *OC-side appointment-eligibility* rule: *"If the manager is to receive a fee or reward … a person is not eligible to be appointed unless the person is a registered manager."* (verified verbatim).
> These are distinct provisions. Use **s 178** for the offence and **Part 12** for the registration regime.

| # | Requirement | Tag | Source |
|---|-------------|-----|--------|

| BLA-2 | A **company may register in its own name**; individual directors do **not** each need to be registered. In a **partnership**, every partner must be individually registered. | [FOUNDER] | CAV *Apply for registration* — How to apply |

## 5. Statutory Trust Accounts (OC Act s 122) — verified verbatim

Full text of s 122(2)–(4) was retrieved and confirmed current (as amended by No. 4/2021). **This resolves the audit's and Track A's "unverified" flag on s 122(2)(c): the separate-bank-account requirement is real and in force.**

| # | Requirement | Tag | Source (s 122, verbatim) |
|---|-------------|-----|--------------------------|
| TR-1 | A manager **holds all money held on behalf of an OC on trust** for that OC. | [FOUNDER] + [SOFTWARE] | s 122(2)(a) |
| TR-2 | Where s 122(3) applies, the manager **must account separately** for the money held for each OC on the plan of subdivision. | [SOFTWARE] | s 122(2)(b) |
| TR-3 | **Subject to s 122(3), the manager must hold all money held on behalf of separate owners corporations on trust in SEPARATE BANK ACCOUNTS.** | [FOUNDER] + [SOFTWARE reconciliation] | **s 122(2)(c)** (verified verbatim) |
| TR-4 | The manager must provide, as soon as practicable on reasonable request, **copies of trust bank-account statements for any period within the 3 years** preceding the request. | [SOFTWARE export] | s 122(2)(d) |
| TR-5 | **Interest earned** on trust money is itself money held on trust for the OC. | [SOFTWARE] | s 122(4) |
| TR-6 | **Pooling exception (only):** money for separate OCs may sit in the **same** account only if (a) each OC is on the *same* plan of subdivision **and** has consented; OR (b) the account is a **statutory trust account** held by a licensed estate agent (Estate Agents Act 1980), an Australian legal practitioner, or a licensed conveyancer (Conveyancers Act 2006). | [REQUIRES: lawyer / accountant] | s 122(3)(a)–(b) |

**Design consequence for GoodStrata:** the current architecture — one shared platform Monoova NPP account with segregation only at the ledger layer — does **not** satisfy s 122(2)(c) (audit blocker P0-1). The two compliant routes are: **(i)** a separate ADI account per OC, or **(ii)** the s 122(3)(b) intermediary route (money held in a solicitor's / estate agent's statutory trust account). Route (ii) also intersects the AFSL question in §6 and must be chosen with counsel.
