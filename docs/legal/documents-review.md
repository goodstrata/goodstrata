# ToS + Privacy Policy — clause-by-clause issues table for counsel

> Prepared as factual/product research for counsel review. Not legal advice.

Prepared 2026-07-05 against the live documents in this repo: `site/terms/index.html` and
`site/privacy/index.html` (both "Last updated July 2026"), cross-checked against the deployed
code paths named below. Companion documents: `docs/LEGAL-BRIEF.md`, `docs/COMPLIANCE-GAPS.md`,
`docs/legal/claims-inventory.md`.

Issue categories used: **s 64** (ACL non-excludable consumer guarantees), **UCT** (unfair
contract terms — standard-form consumer/small-business contracts, civil penalties since
Nov 2023), **s 18** (misleading or deceptive conduct), **APP n** (Australian Privacy
Principles), **Gap** (something promised or assumed that the document or the code doesn't
deliver).

## Part 1 — Terms of Service (`site/terms/index.html`)

(`terms:76-78`) | Formation | An OC is bound by resolution, not by an individual clicking sign-up. A committee member creating an account may not actually bind the OC; the deemed-authority warranty may not cure that. | What acceptance mechanics validly bind an OC (committee resolution evidence at onboarding?), and what is the fallback position if the individual lacked authority? |

| T3 | Cl 1: "We may add, change, or remove features over time. Where a change materially reduces the core functionality you rely on, we will give reasonable notice." (`terms:100-103`) | UCT | Unilateral right to degrade the service, with the only protection being self-judged "reasonable notice". On a free service this may be defensible, but it interacts badly with "free forever" marketing (see claims inventory C8, C23). | Is a unilateral feature-removal right with notice-only protection fair under the UCT regime for a small-business OC counterparty, and does it need a termination-for-convenience counter-right or data-export guarantee to balance it? |
| T4 | Cl 3: "a small, disclosed processing margin may apply … Any such margin is shown before a payment flow is used." (`terms:123`) | Gap / s 18 | **The disclosure UX this clause promises does not exist yet.** Payments are sandbox-only (Monoova driver in progress, `docs/LEGAL-BRIEF.md` §B; internal task #22). No margin amount is implemented or published anywhere in the codebase or on the site, yet the site repeatedly calls the margin "published" (claims inventory C12). | What margin-disclosure artefacts (amount, timing, per-transaction display) must exist before the first live payment, and should cl 3 state the actual figure or link to a published schedule? |
| T5 | Cl 3 generally — **no "not a bank / no funds held" clause exists in the live ToS.** `docs/LEGAL-BRIEF.md` (§B item 5) tells counsel to "Review the 'GoodStrata is not a bank / does not hold funds' ToS clause (drafted, unreviewed)" — but no such clause appears anywhere in `site/terms/index.html` (grep for "bank"/"funds"/"trust" returns nothing). | Gap | The clause the brief says was drafted never shipped. | T8 | Cl 5 + cl 12: "We may suspend or limit access where we reasonably believe use breaches these terms or puts the service, its data, or other users at risk." (`terms:162-163`, `terms:240-244`) | UCT | Suspension on self-judged "reasonable belief", with notice only "where practical and lawful". There is no committed data-export or hand-back obligation on suspension/termination — despite the marketing promising "export your data … the licence guarantees it, forever". | Should there be a contractual data-export/retrieval right on suspension or termination (format, time window), and is the suspension right sufficiently balanced for UCT purposes? |
| T9 | Cl 6: "data for the hosted service is stored in Australia" (`terms:170-171`) | s 18 / APP 8 | Storage (Supabase Sydney, S3/SES defaulted to ap-southeast-2 in `deploy/app/worker.ts:72,87`) is Australian, but **processing** is not: hosted AI prompts route to Anthropic (US) when `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` is set (`worker.ts:57-58`; default model `anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-5`, `packages/agents/src/models.ts:89`), otherwise to OpenRouter (US) (`worker.ts:59-65`); the estimator sends uploaded documents to Google Gemini via OpenRouter (`estimator.ts:32`); compute runs in Cloudflare containers not region-pinned; Daily.co handles committee video (`worker.ts:93-95`). | T10 | Cl 6: "you grant us the rights we need to host, process, back up, and display that data" (`terms:173-174`) | Drafting | An undefined, open-ended licence ("the rights we need"). Fine in spirit; counsel may want it scoped (purpose-limited, non-exclusive, ends on termination except audit-log retention). | Should the data licence be expressly scoped and its survival on termination reconciled with the append-only audit log retained "forever" (P7 below)? |
| T11 | Cl 8: "Their availability and their own terms are outside our control, and we are not responsible for their acts or omissions beyond what the law requires of us." (`terms:192-194`) | s 64 | s 274/ s 64: a supplier generally can't disclaim guarantee liability merely because a subcontractor caused the failure. The tail "beyond what the law requires" saves it technically but the sentence reads as a broad disclaimer. | Is the third-party disclaimer effective and appropriately worded given guarantees apply to the whole supplied service including subcontracted components (payments, email, video)? |
| T12 | Cl 9: "provided 'as is' and 'as available' … we make no warranties of any kind" followed by the ACL savings clause and a re-supply limitation "at our option" (`terms:198-212`) | s 64 / s 64A | Two questions: (a) whether the free service attracts consumer guarantees at all (LEGAL-BRIEF Q11 assumes it does — worth confirming, since "free" complicates the s 3 consumer test); (b) whether the s 64A re-supply limitation is even available — it is **not** available for services "of a kind ordinarily acquired for personal, domestic or household use", and strata administration for residential owners is arguably exactly that. | Do consumer guarantees attach to the free hosted tier, and is the s 64A re-supply limitation available for this service type — or is cl 9's second paragraph ineffective for residential OCs? |
| T13 | Cl 10: liability cap = greater of 12-month fees "and (b) one hundred Australian dollars (AUD $100)" plus full consequential-loss exclusion including "loss of … data" (`terms:214-228`) | UCT / s 64 | A $100 cap on a platform that runs an OC's finances and statutory records; loss-of-data exclusion sits badly with the platform being the OC's system of record. Post-2023 UCT penalties make an over-reaching cap a live risk even if never enforced. | Is a $100/12-month-fees cap defensible for a free service under UCT and s 64, and should categories like data loss, trust-money errors, or statutory-record corruption be carved out or insured instead of excluded? |
| T14 | Cl 11: indemnity for "your breach of these terms, your unlawful use … except to the extent the claim is caused by our own breach or negligence" (`terms:230-236`) | UCT | The carve-out helps, but a consumer/small-business indemnity in a standard-form free contract is still a classic UCT review item (one-sided; no mutual indemnity from Good Strata). | Is the indemnity necessary at all on a free service, and if kept, does UCT require it be narrowed (e.g. third-party claims only, proportionate reduction)? |
| T15 | Cl 13: "We may update these terms from time to time … Continued use after a change takes effect means you accept the updated terms." (`terms:247-253`) | UCT | Unilateral variation with acceptance-by-conduct is on the ACCC's list of typically-unfair terms. "Reasonable notice" only for changes that "materially affect your rights", self-assessed. | What variation mechanism survives UCT scrutiny (advance notice period, right to terminate and export before effect, no retrospective changes to fees/liability)? |
| T16 | Cl 14: Victoria governing law, non-exclusive jurisdiction (`terms:255-260`) | Low | Sensible. Note the product markets nationally while `for-owners` content is Victoria-specific; strata legislation differs by state. | None beyond confirming non-exclusive jurisdiction is intended; consider a plain note that state strata law varies. |
| T17 | Cl 15 / contact: "reach us via goodstrata.com.au or through our repository at github.com/goodstrata/goodstrata" (`terms:262-268`) | Gap | No email address, phone, or postal address anywhere in the ToS. GitHub issues are public — inviting legal/account correspondence there is inappropriate. | What contact channels must a consumer-facing ToS provide, and should a monitored legal/notices email address be stated? |
| T18 | **Missing clauses** (whole document) | Gap | No clause on: funds handling (T5); data export / exit assistance (contradicts "no exit charge — ever" and "export your data" marketing); service levels / support (none promised — fine, but say so); dispute resolution before courts; assignment/novation on sale of business (the privacy policy contemplates a sale, `privacy:182-184`, the ToS doesn't); entire agreement; force majeure. | Which of these does counsel consider mandatory before launch, in particular funds handling and exit/export? |
| T19 | Footer of the ToS page itself: "This page is a reasonable starting draft … should have it reviewed by a qualified Australian lawyer before relying on it." (`terms:271-275`) | s 18 / optics | The live, public ToS announces it is an unreviewed draft. That candour is admirable but undermines the document's contractual force and invites argument that users weren't bound. | Should this disclaimer be removed once counsel settles the terms — and does its presence today weaken enforceability of anything above it? |

## Part 2 — Privacy Policy (`site/privacy/index.html`)

| # | Clause / quote | Category | Why counsel should look | Suggested question for counsel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Intro: "We are committed to handling personal information in line with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)" (`privacy:75-77`) | APP | Good Strata likely sits under the $3M small-business threshold but voluntarily opts in (LEGAL-BRIEF Q7). Opting in publicly makes APP compliance enforceable expectation-wise even if not strictly required. | Confirm the opt-in posture and whether the trading-in-personal-information or other exceptions independently pull Good Strata into the Act regardless of turnover. |
| P2 | Cl 3: "the model that powers the agents may run locally or through a model provider engaged to process operational data on our behalf under appropriate confidentiality and security terms" (`privacy:128-132`) | APP 5/8 / s 18 | The model providers are unnamed. In production they are Anthropic (US) — precedence in `deploy/app/worker.ts:57-58` — and/or OpenRouter (US) routing to third-party model hosts incl. Google (`estimator.ts:32`, `worker.ts:59-65`). "May run locally" is true only for self-hosters; the hosted service the policy governs never runs models locally. APP 8 requires reasonable steps + practically a disclosure of likely overseas recipients/countries. | Must the policy name the AI providers and their countries, and what contractual controls (DPA, no-training, retention limits) does APP 8/APP 11 require with Anthropic, OpenRouter and downstream model hosts? |
| P3 | Cl 3: "We do not use your personal information to train third-party AI models." (`privacy:131-132`) | s 18 | Good Strata doesn't train models — but the sentence will be read as "your data is not used for AI training". Whether OpenRouter and its downstream hosts (e.g. Google for the estimator's Gemini default) log or train on API traffic depends on their tier/settings, which the repo does not evidence. | Can this representation be verified and contractually locked for every provider in the chain (Anthropic API, OpenRouter routing settings, Google), and should it be reworded to what Good Strata can actually warrant? |
| P4 | Cl 4: "Our database and application data are hosted on Supabase infrastructure in the Sydney region, and document and file storage uses Amazon S3." (`privacy:137-140`) | Accuracy / s 18 | Two inaccuracies: (a) the **application** does not run on Supabase — it runs in Cloudflare containers (`deploy/app/worker.ts`, `@cloudflare/containers`), whose placement is not region-pinned; only Postgres is Supabase Sydney. (b) The S3 region is unstated in the policy; code defaults to ap-southeast-2 (`worker.ts:87`) but the policy should say so if "stored in Australia" is the headline. Cloudflare is not disclosed anywhere in the policy. | Correct the hosting description; must Cloudflare (edge/compute) be listed as a recipient, and does non-region-pinned compute over Australian data engage APP 8? |
| P5 | Cl 5 third-party table lists only Supabase, Monoova, AWS SES, AWS S3 (`privacy:148-180`) | APP 5/8 / Gap | The table omits at least: the **AI model provider(s)** (Anthropic / OpenRouter / Google — operational data and estimator documents); **Daily.co** (committee video calls and meeting transcription — `worker.ts:93-95` and the Daily transcription feature in recent commits; US company; audio/voice data); **Cloudflare** (all traffic and compute); **Google** (Tag Manager on every marketing page incl. this policy page, `privacy:4-10`; and Google OAuth sign-in, `worker.ts:97-103`). | Which of these are APP 6 disclosures vs APP 8 cross-border disclosures, and does meeting audio/transcription via Daily require its own consent flow (see P10)? |
| P6 | Cl 5: "Some providers may process or store data outside Australia; where that occurs, we take reasonable steps…" (`privacy:185-188`) | s 18 / APP 8 | This hedge quietly contradicts the headline "Data for the hosted service is stored in Australia" (cl 4) and flatly contradicts the app's signup-page claim "Your data stays in Australia" (`apps/web/src/routes/signup.tsx:37-38`). The policy knows data leaves; the signup page says it doesn't. | Settle one consistent formulation across policy, ToS cl 6, and the signup page — what qualification does APP 8 and s 18 require? |
| P7 | Cl 7: "Because the audit log is append-only, entries in it are retained as part of the corporation's permanent record." (`privacy:204-207`) | APP 11.2 | Personal information embedded in event-log entries can never be deleted or de-identified — by design (DB trigger blocks UPDATE/DELETE, `packages/db/migrations/0001_event_log_triggers.sql`). APP 11.2 requires destruction/de-identification when no longer needed; "permanent by design" needs a justification (legal obligation? OC record?) that may not cover all entry types (e.g. a resident's maintenance-request free text). | Is permanent retention of personal information in an append-only log defensible under APP 11.2, and is a redaction/tombstoning mechanism (payload encryption with key destruction, pointer redaction) needed for deletion requests? |
| P8 | Cl 8 access/correction: "Some corrections in a strata context are made through the owners corporation or its committee, and we will help route your request appropriately." (`privacy:217-218`) | APP 12/13 | Reasonable, but the controller/processor split is undefined: for OC records, is Good Strata holding the information "on behalf of" the OC (who then owes the APP 12/13 duties) or in its own right? The policy takes both postures at different points. | Characterise the Good Strata ↔ OC relationship (agent/processor vs APP-entity in its own right) and align the access/correction clause with it. |
| P9 | Cl 11 contact: complaints "via goodstrata.com.au or through our repository at github.com" (`privacy:236-245`) | APP 1.3 / Gap | A privacy complaint filed as a **public GitHub issue** would itself publish the complainant's personal information. No email, form, phone or postal channel is given. OAIC expects an accessible, confidential complaints channel. | What minimum contact/complaint channels must be stated (dedicated privacy email at least), and should a privacy officer be named? |
| P10 | **Missing content** (whole document) | Gap | No mention of: cookies/analytics — **Google Tag Manager runs on every marketing page including this policy** (`privacy:4-10`) with zero disclosure and no consent banner; meeting **video/audio recording and AI transcription** (Daily + the "AI Chair" conductor — engages Surveillance Devices Act 1999 (Vic) consent for recorded conversations); **sensitive information** potentially captured in grievance/complaint records (`packages/db/src/schema/grievances.ts` — health, disputes); anonymity/pseudonymity option (APP 2); direct marketing (APP 7); government identifiers; how Google OAuth sign-in data is handled. | Which of these are mandatory inclusions, and does AI meeting recording/transcription need express in-flow consent (each participant, each meeting) rather than policy-level disclosure? |
| P11 | Footer: "This policy is a reasonable starting draft … should have it reviewed by a qualified Australian privacy lawyer before relying on it." (`privacy:249-253`) | Optics | Same issue as T19 — the live policy self-describes as an unreviewed draft. | Remove once settled; confirm no interim exposure from publicly operating under a self-declared draft. |
