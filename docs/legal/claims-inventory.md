## Where the estimator's numbers come from (asked specifically)

Two different number sources, worth keeping distinct for counsel:

1. **The slider/receipt figures** ($8,400, $6,600, $1,320, $480, "$700/lot average") are
   hard-coded arithmetic in page JavaScript — `lots × $550 / $110 / $40 / $700`
   (`site/index.html:224-227`, `site/what-am-i-paying/index.html:219-223`). They are a
   modelled illustration with **no cited source in the repo** (C4/C5).
2. **The upload tool's figures** come from a vision LLM reading the user's own document —
   default model `google/gemini-2.5-flash` via OpenRouter
   (`packages/agents/src/estimator.ts:32`), extracting manager-fee line items to a Zod
   schema; deterministic code only sums parts and derives per-lot
   (`estimator.ts:264-288`). Nothing is persisted (`apps/api/src/routes/estimator.ts:9-10`),
   but the document transits US-based AI providers (C24).

## Top-5 items counsel must see first

1. **"Your data stays in Australia" vs actual data flows (C9, C24, P2–P6).** Signup-page
   claim contradicted by Anthropic-US agent routing, the estimator sending user documents to
   OpenRouter/Google, Daily.co meeting audio/transcription, and non-region-pinned Cloudflare
   compute — while the privacy policy's provider table lists none of them and misdescribes
   where the application runs.
2. **The "$0 / free" comparison architecture (C2, C3, C15, C8).** "With GoodStrata $0/yr"
   against an $8,400 strawman while revenue is a margin on the OC's own levy flows; the
   margin is called "published" but no figure is published or implemented, and the payment
   claims are present-tense while payments are sandbox-only.
3. **The missing funds-handling clause (documents-review T5, T4).** The "not a bank / no
   funds held" clause the legal brief says was drafted is absent from the live ToS, and the
   margin-disclosure UX promised by ToS cl 3 doesn't exist — both must land before Monoova
   goes live.
4. **The liability stack on a free consumer service (T12–T15).** $100 cap, as-is framing,
   one-sided indemnity, unilateral variation — classic UCT/penalty and s 64/s 64A exposure,
   with the added question of whether the re-supply limitation is even available for
   residential strata services.
5. **Proof-claim hygiene: "real" vs "seeded" and "$0.00 by an LLM" (C12a, C13, C14).** Two
   pages call the seeded demo building "real"; the blanket "no money computed by an LLM"
   claim collides with the LLM-extracted estimator figures; and "even we can't edit the log"
   overstates what a self-administered DB trigger guarantees. All cheap to fix, all bad in a
   regulator's screenshot.
